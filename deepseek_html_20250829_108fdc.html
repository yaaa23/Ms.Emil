Procedure Text: Giving Tips

English Grade 10 - Mastering the Art of Giving Advice

Learning Objective

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to write clear and effective procedure texts in the form of tips using appropriate imperative sentences and logical sequencing.

What is Procedure Text (Tips)?

A procedure text is a text that explains how to do something or gives advice in a series of steps. When writing tips, we use imperative sentences (commands or instructions) to clearly convey advice.

Structure

Clear goal followed by sequential steps

Language Features

Imperative verbs, simple present tense

Purpose

To instruct or advise how to do something

Example: Tips for Effective Studying

  1. Find a quiet place to study without distractions.
  2. Prepare all your materials before starting your study session.
  3. Create a study schedule and stick to it consistently.
  4. Take short breaks every 30–45 minutes to maintain focus.
  5. Review your notes regularly to reinforce learning.

Note: These tips use imperative verbs (find, prepare, create, take, review) to give clear instructions.
